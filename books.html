Book Reviews

The Rational Optimist by Matt Ridley

the Rational Optimist is a great book for anyone who wants to feel, well, optimistic. Matt Ridley details how our world has improved and peoples lives have gotten better over the last few hundred years.

The two main points of the book are:

1.   We are constantly hearing negative predictions about impending doom and catastrophe but if you examine these consistently occurring claims against the events of the last few centuries you will find they are consistently wrong. In general, each generation is are happier, healthier and wealthier than the previous one.

2.   This trend towards improvement is driven by the exchange of ideas and goods between groups. Essentially Matt argues that what drives human progress is trade. He cites examples of the benefits of mixing ideas from different cultures and also of societies that became closed off and seemed to slip backwards in technology and quality of life.

After reading this book I felt genuinely optimistic about humanities future and our ability to deal with upcoming challenges. If Matt's argument regarding idea exchange and trade have any merit then the positive trends we have been seeing will be greatly accelerated by increasing access to the internet.

Because this book was published over 10 years ago it gives us a chance to test Matt's theories against reality (albeit over a relatively short time horizon) and it would seem they hold true. Specialization was a key idea throughout as what is so magic about trade and we have seen more of that through Etsy, Udemy and many other areas of the world wide web.

One area Matt fails to mention where we have seen huge gains is in resource allocation through new technology. Whether it's airBnB, Uber, Craigslist or the many other marketplaces and platforms on the web. The new connectedness of society has allowed us to get much more use from our existing stuff.

The book is a must read at the very least to counterbalance the constant barrage of doomsday predictions we receive. You will also learn some history and evolutionary psychology as a bonus.
